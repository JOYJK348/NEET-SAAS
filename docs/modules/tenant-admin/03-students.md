# Student Management

## Module Overview

The Student Management module is responsible for managing the complete student lifecycle within the coaching institute.

This module begins from a student's initial enquiry and continues throughout admission, parent association, academic enrollment, learning activities, performance tracking, and finally course completion.

The objective of this module is to provide a structured, seamless, and well-organized academic journey for every student while ensuring effective collaboration between the institute, tutors, students, and parents.

---

# Objectives

- Manage the complete student lifecycle.
- Simplify the admission process.
- Maintain student academic records.
- Associate students with parents or guardians.
- Organize students into appropriate courses and batches.
- Monitor attendance and academic performance.
- Improve communication between the institute, students and parents.
- Support long-term academic tracking.

---

# Student Lifecycle

The complete student journey consists of the following stages.

---

## 1. Student Enquiry

A prospective student expresses interest in joining the institute.

Purpose

- Capture enquiry details.
- Understand course requirements.
- Begin the admission process.

Status

- Enquiry

---

## 2. Admission

The student officially joins the institute.

Purpose

- Verify student information.
- Verify parent or guardian information.
- Complete the admission process.

Status

- Admission Confirmed

---

## 3. Parent Association

Every student should be associated with one or more parents or guardians.

Purpose

- Enable Parent Portal access.
- Improve parent communication.
- Monitor student progress.
- Support attendance and performance tracking.

Business Rule

- A parent may be associated with multiple students.
- Every student should have at least one parent or guardian.

---

## 4. Course Enrollment

The student is enrolled in the appropriate academic program.

Examples

- Regular Course
- Advanced Program
- Crash Course
- Foundation Program

Purpose

- Define the student's academic journey.
- Organize curriculum delivery.

---

## 5. Batch Allocation

The student is assigned to one or more batches.

Purpose

- Academic organization.
- Tutor assignment.
- Timetable generation.
- Live class scheduling.

---

## 6. Student Account Provisioning

The system prepares the student's digital learning account.

Purpose

- Generate Student ID.
- Create login credentials.
- Enable Student Portal access.
- Send welcome communication.

Parent account provisioning may also be completed during this stage if required.

---

## 7. Learning Journey

The student begins learning through the platform.

Activities

- Attend Classes
- Join Live Classes
- Access Study Materials
- Watch Recorded Classes
- Complete Assignments
- Practice Questions

Purpose

- Support continuous learning.
- Improve examination preparation.

---

## 8. Attendance Monitoring

The institute continuously tracks student attendance.

Purpose

- Monitor attendance.
- Identify irregular students.
- Notify parents when necessary.

---

## 9. Assessments

Students participate in periodic assessments.

Activities

- Mock Tests
- Chapter Tests
- Weekly Tests
- Grand Tests
- Assignments

Purpose

- Measure academic progress.
- Improve examination readiness.

---

## 10. Evaluation

Tutors evaluate student performance.

Methods

- Manual Paper Evaluation
- OMR Evaluation
- Online Evaluation (Future)

Purpose

- Prepare results.
- Measure learning outcomes.
- Provide academic feedback.

---

## 11. Performance Tracking

Student performance is monitored throughout the course.

Examples

- Test Scores
- Subject-wise Performance
- Attendance Percentage
- Rank
- Accuracy
- Weak Areas
- Strong Areas
- Progress Trends

Purpose

- Track academic growth.
- Identify improvement opportunities.

---

## 12. Parent Engagement

Parents continuously monitor student progress through the Parent Portal.

Examples

- Attendance
- Test Results
- Academic Performance
- Announcements
- Learning Progress

Purpose

- Improve parent involvement.
- Encourage student accountability.
- Strengthen institute-parent communication.

---

## 13. Course Completion

The student successfully completes the enrolled course.

Possible Status

- Active
- Completed
- Dropped
- Alumni

Purpose

- Preserve academic history.
- Support future reference.

# Business Rules

- Every student belongs to a course.
- Every student belongs to one or more batches.
- Every student should have at least one parent or guardian.
- A parent may be linked with multiple students.
- Every student receives a unique Student ID.
- Students can access only assigned academic resources.
- Students can attend only assigned batches and live classes.
- Academic performance should be recorded throughout the course.
- Completed students should remain available for historical records.
